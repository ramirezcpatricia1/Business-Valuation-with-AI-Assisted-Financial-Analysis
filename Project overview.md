## Project Overview

This project presents a financial diagnostic and sell-side readiness assessment for an insurance agency.

The objective was to analyze the company's historical financial information, evaluate the quality and sustainability of its earnings, identify potential buyer concerns, and develop recommendations to improve the company's transaction readiness.

The project combines financial analysis performed in Microsoft Excel with AI-assisted research, identification of financial indicators, review of key findings, and document drafting.

## My Role

I was responsible for:

- Reviewing and organizing the financial information.
- Analyzing the income statement and balance sheet.
- Calculating year-over-year variations and financial indicators.
- Reviewing revenue and expense composition.
- Identifying non-recurring and owner-related transactions.
- Evaluating producer economics and compensation.
- Analyzing customer and producer concentration.
- Developing a preliminary adjusted EBITDA calculation.
- Preparing the financial diagnostic and sell-side readiness recommendations.
- Reviewing and validating the final conclusions.

## Analysis Performed

The Excel workbook includes analysis of:

- Income statements for FY2023–FY2025.
- Balance sheets for FY2023–FY2025.
- Revenue composition and year-over-year growth.
- Expense variation and materiality.
- Property and casualty commission income.
- Contingent commission income.
- Top customer concentration.
- Monthly bank activity.
- Producer economics and compensation.
- Owner compensation and related-party transactions.
- Non-recurring income and expenses.
- Preliminary EBITDA normalization.

## Key Findings

- Total revenue increased significantly in FY2025, but part of the growth was supported by non-recurring income.
- Contingent commissions included a one-time carrier signing and onboarding bonus.
- Other income included a one-time referral fee.
- Several technology, marketing, legal, and owner-related expenses required normalization review.
- The owner was also the company's largest producer, creating key-person and revenue portability risk.
- The acquired book of business showed weaker-than-expected retention.
- Certain compensation, equity, acquisition, and contingent commission balances required additional supporting documentation.
- The company appeared valuable and profitable but was not fully prepared for an immediate sale process.

## Preliminary Adjusted EBITDA

The analysis developed a preliminary EBITDA normalization bridge that:

1. Started with reported operating income.
2. Added back depreciation and amortization.
3. Removed identified non-recurring income.
4. Added back selected one-time, unusual, discretionary, and owner-related expenses.

The resulting preliminary adjusted EBITDA was approximately **$3.257 million**, subject to further review of owner compensation and related-party rent.

## Recommendations

The final recommendations included:

- Implementing a stronger monthly financial close process.
- Preparing a formal EBITDA normalization schedule.
- Improving documentation for revenue and receivables.
- Creating equity, debt, and amortization schedules.
- Tracking retention by producer and line of business.
- Separating owner-related and related-party transactions.
- Organizing a buyer-ready due diligence file.
- Preparing the company over a 12–24 month period before starting a formal sale process.
